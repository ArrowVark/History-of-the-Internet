History of the Internet

ARPANET

1969

ARPANET was created to connect academic and research institutions with the benefit of being able to withstand a nuclear attack. This was the start of the Internet as we know it today, as this was the first time packet-switching technology was being used. Without ARPANET, there is the chance the internet would look completly different, or even exist at all!

Introduction of Email

1971

Ray Tomlinson created the first networked email system, bringing forth the "@" symbol to seperate the user's name from the computer's name. This was later followed up by Gary Thuerk who sent the first mass email in 1978, marking the beginning of email marketing.

Adoption of TCP/IP

1983

ARPANET adopted the TCP/IP protocol suite, laying the groundwork for the modern internet. TCP/IP is still being used today for end-to-end data delivery and is unlikely to be replaced anytime soon.
