History of the Internet

ARPANET

1969

ARPANET was created to connect academic and research institutions with the benefit of being able to withstand a nuclear attack. This was the start of the Internet as we know it today, as this was the first time packet-switching technology was being used. Without ARPANET, there is the chance the internet would look completely different, or even exist at all!

Introduction of Email

1971

Ray Tomlinson created the first networked email system, bringing forth the "@" symbol to separate the user's name from the computer's name. This was later followed up by Gary Thuerk who sent the first mass email in 1978, marking the beginning of email marketing.

Adoption of TCP/IP

1983

ARPANET adopted the TCP/IP protocol suite, laying the groundwork for the modern internet. TCP/IP is still being used today for end-to-end data delivery and is unlikely to be replaced anytime soon.

Proposal for the World Wide Web

1989

Tim Berners-Lee, a British scientist who worked at CERN, proposed for the creation of a system to link together hypertext documents via the internet. This would further be developed and become the World Wide Web as we know it today.

Launch of the First Website

1991

Tim Burners-Lee later created the first-ever website, meant to explain the concept of the World Wide Web and how to use it. In August, the same month he created his website, his web browser became publicly available. By the end of 1992, there were ten websites.

Introduction of the Mosaic Browser

1993

This was the launch of the first graphical web browser, making the web accessible to non-technical users. With Mosaic's introduction, web traffic increased more than 10,000 times within the span of one year. Ths inspired the creation of other browsers like Netscape and Firefox, permanently transforming global communication and commerce.

Launch of Yahoo!

1994

Yahoo! was made to be a web directory and search engine for the young web, created by electrical engineers at Stanford University. Yahoo! became one of the first major internet portals. Yahoo is an acronym for "Yet Another Hierarchical Officious Oracle," chosen because it reflected the Wild West nature of the internet.

Commercialization of the Internet

1995

This marks the beginning of the commercialization that exists today on the internet. This started with the creation and management of online services solely for financial gain. This is seen today in e-commerce, online marketing, and paid services to name a few.

Founding of Google

1998

Larry Page and Sergey Brin founded a revolutionary search engine, known at the time for its PageRank algorithm. Google reshaped the web and has become a dominant force on the internet.

Launch of Wikipedia

2001

With Wikipedia's launch, anyone with internet access could edit the online encyclopedia. This has revolutionized how people get information online. One one hand, it is easier than ever to find information on nearly any topic online, but because of its open nature, people have had to learn not to trust everything they read online.

Launch of Facebook

2004

When Facebook was introduced, it revolutionized the way people communicated, allowing people to easily connect with others through the internet. People with shared interests could gather together and post effortlessly to the whole group.

Launch of YouTube

2005

The launch of YouTube revolutionized how people find quick information on the internet. YouTube also allowed people to find many different genres of videos such as entertainment, vlog, and how-to. YouTube has since provided many people with large followings a full time job that pays them just as much or even more than a regular job would.

Introduction of the iPhone

2007

With the introduction of the iPhone, people could easily access the internet on a device that fits in the palm of their hand. With their innovative touch screen design and introduction of mobile apps, Apple forever shaped how people access the internet.

Launch of Instagram

2010

With smartphones being a recently new introduction at this time, the idea of sharing photos in a social media format appealed to many people. Within the first month of its launch there were already 1 Million users, and 10 Million after just a year!

Adoption of HTTPS by Major Websites

2015

With HTTPS, a secure connection is created between the server and the user. This is important because without it, people with malicious intent could intercept data going to and from a server.
